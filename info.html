Practical Information
for your stay in Vitry-sur-Seine

Accessibility

Vitry-sur-Seine is well-connected to Paris and surrounding cities by a wide range of buses and trams. It is wildy accessible through buses such as the 180, 132, and 172, making it easy to explore the Parisian suburbs.
The 180 bus will take you directly to the metro at the stop Villejuif-Louis-Aragon (line 7) or Liberté (line 8). The tramway T9 also goes through the city and will take you to the 13th arrondissement of Paris, where it is easy to travel further into the capital thanks to tramway T3a and metro line 7. Additionally, the T9 and 180 bus connect to several RER C stops, which run through Paris and its surrounding areas. Both the T9 and RER C lines are visible on the map.
For those driving, the city is accessible via the A86 highway and various departmental roads.

Weather and Best Time to Visit

Vitry-sur-Seine has a warm and temperate climate, though with significant rainfall throughout the year. Here are the average temperatures depending on the seasons.
Winter (December to February): Average temperatures range from 1.3°C to 7.9°C.
Spring (March to May): Temperatures increase, averaging between 7.7°C and 19.3°C.
Summer (June to August): Warm, rarely cloudy, with average highs around 24.7°C. Summer 2024, highs reached 37°C.
Autumn (September to November): Slowly cooling, with temperatures ranging from 14.8°C to 20.9°C. CITY-FACTS.COM

Best Time to Visit: Vitry-sur-Seine can be visited during the whole year. But the optimal period is from mid-May to mid-September as the weather is suitable for outdoor activites.
